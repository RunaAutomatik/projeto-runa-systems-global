# MIND$ — Product Requirements Document

> **Tagline:** Extract the expert. Build the brain. Scale the knowledge.
> **Position in teia:** Low ticket → follows CREATOR$ → upsell to $QUAD

---

## The Big Idea

MIND$ teaches how to extract intellectual property — cognitive patterns, mental frameworks, decision processes — from any expert source and build an AI knowledge base (a "second brain") that can be embedded into an agent, packaged as a product, or used to power a squad.

**Two extraction paths:**
1. **Reference route:** Extract from external expert references (e.g., Alex Hormozi for offer-building, a specific philosopher for worldview, a practitioner for methodology) — same technique used to build ARES in this project
2. **Self route:** The expert/mentor/consultant extracts their own methodology, mental models, and cognitive biases — turning their head into a transferable asset

The knowledge extracted here is the "brain" that goes inside the avatar created in CREATOR$ — and into any agent, product, SaaS, or service offering.

---

## Core Promise

> "By the end of MIND$, you'll have a structured knowledge base that represents how you (or your reference) thinks — ready to be embedded into an AI agent, packaged into a product, or used to train your entire squad."

---

## Who It's For (ICP)

**Primary:** Experts, mentors, consultants, and creators who want to transform their knowledge into a scalable asset — or leverage the mental models of proven references to power their agents.

**Dual application:**
- **Reference route:** "I want my agent to think like [expert X]" — extract external cognitive patterns
- **Self route:** "I want to clone my own reasoning" — extract the expert's own IP

**Why this matters (the RUNA method):** Knowledge is a flexible asset. Once extracted and structured, it can be packaged as a product, embedded in an agent, turned into a SaaS, or sold as a framework. The same extraction is the raw material for infinite outputs.
