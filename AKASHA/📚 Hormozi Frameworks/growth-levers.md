# Growth Levers — Alex Hormozi

## The 2 Fundamental Ways to Grow Revenue

> "There are two ways to grow a business from a fundamental standpoint: number one, you get more customers; number two, you make those customers worth more. That's it."

Everything else is a derivation of these two.

---

## The 4 Steps: $0 to $10 Million

1. **Learn to sell one product** — achieve a single sale
2. **Build a consistent sales process** → gets you to $1M
3. **Sell a second product to existing customers** → gets you to ~$5M
4. **Sell on multiple channels** → gets you to $10M

> "Zero to one million: one product, one avatar, one channel. That's it."

---

## The 3 Phases of Scaling

> "Zero to a million is promotion. One to ten million is product. Ten to a hundred is people."

### Phase 1: Promotion ($0 → $1M)
Generate traction. Get people to find you. The goal here is to validate that someone will buy.

### Phase 2: Product ($1M → $10M)
**This is where most businesses make the mistake.** After getting first revenue, entrepreneurs dump more money into promotion instead of fixing the product. The correct move:
- Achieve product-market fit
- Make the product exceptional
- Generate virality, reviews, referrals

> "Once you get the traction, the whole point is to get product-market fit and make the product exceptional. Most people then try to dump more promotion on top but the product isn't fixed."

When product is excellent, every dollar of advertising returns dramatically more. That's when scaling promotion becomes highly leveraged.

### Phase 3: People ($10M → $100M)
Build the team to systemize and scale what's working.

## 6 Ways to Increase Lifetime Value (LTV)

Hormozi redefines LTV as **Lifetime Gross Profit (LTGP)** — the gross profit generated over a customer's lifetime, not just revenue.

| # | Lever | Explanation |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Increase price** | Price itself signals value; raises LTGP without changing costs |
| 2 | **Decrease fulfillment cost** | Same delivery at lower cost = higher gross margin |
| 3 | **Increase purchase frequency** | Get customers to buy again and again |
| 4 | **Upsell** | Sell a bigger/better version of the same thing (Honda → Lexus) |
| 5 | **Cross-sell** | Sell an adjacent need (burger with the soda) |
| 6 | **Extend contract length** | Keep customers longer through better retention |

> "Sometimes cross-sales are where the majority of profit happens — loss leaders get you in, the next purchase is where the money is."

### The "Bigger Head, Longer Tail" Principle
> "The bigger the head, the longer the tail."

The larger the upfront payment relative to monthly cost, the stickier the customer. A $5,000 down payment + $99/month is far more sticky than $99/month alone — because the customer has already committed psychologically and financially.

This upfront payment also: covers acquisition cost, pays sales commission, funds onboarding.

## Lead Generation Principles

### The Starving Crowd Principle
> "When the football game lets out and everyone's starving and drunk, you want to be the guy at the hot dog stand. You could have the worst hot dogs at wildly overpriced prices and still sell out."

Finding the right market matters more than having the best product or the best sales skills. Supply/demand in your favor beats operational excellence every time.

### The Pareto Filter for Ideal Customers
> "Take your top 100 clients. Look at the top 20 — they're responsible for 80% of revenue. What do they all have in common? Now only sell to that type of person."

Narrowing to best-fit clients with same operational load = 5x revenue.

### The Market Expansion Framework (4 Directions)
When you feel you've maxed out your market, you haven't — you just need to expand:

1. **Up-market** — sell to bigger versions of the same clients (chains instead of single salons; enterprise instead of SMB)
2. **Down-market** — sell to smaller versions (individual stylists instead of salon owners)
3. **Adjacent market** — similar avatar with similar needs (lash salons instead of hair salons)
4. **Broader market** — umbrella over all adjacent (all "beauty" instead of just "hair")

> "I get on with entrepreneurs who say 'I think I've saturated my market' and they're at $2M in a $60B industry."

### The Ad Spend Insight
More spend does not mean better efficiency — it means worse efficiency per dollar. A competitor spending 5x more may be making 2x revenue while converting at 40% of your rate. The game is **who can make the most money**, not who converts best.

> "At some point all your stuff will get less efficient in terms of spend — but that's part of scaling."

## Retention Principles

### Why Retention > Acquisition
Halving churn doubles LTV without spending more on acquisition. The math is unambiguous: reducing churn by 50% increases LTGP by more than 2x because you save acquisition costs.

### Why Lowering Price Does NOT Reduce Churn
Hormozi's definitive lesson:
- Lowered price 25% to reduce churn
- Churn remained completely unchanged
- Lost $5M/year in top-line revenue
- Lost $1M additional from new overhead added simultaneously

> "Most times when you lower your prices you make a lot less money."

Churn is a product/fulfillment problem, not a pricing problem. Fix delivery; never cut price.

### The Conviction → Retention Loop
Higher price → more emotional investment → better results → lower churn → higher LTGP.

## The 4 Vehicles for a Service Business to Scale

| Vehicle | Control | Capital Required | Operational Drag | Enterprise Value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Private Chain** | Full | High (owner fronts all) | High | High |
| **Franchise** | Partial | Low (franchisee pays) | Medium | High (if >100 units) |
| **Licensing** | Partial | Low | Low | Medium |
| **Tech-Enabled / Software** | Variable | High upfront | Very low at scale | Very high |

> "90-95% of franchises never get past 100 locations — and you need 100+ to make real money in that model."

> "If you're not already great at software and don't have a software co-founder — do not try to outsource it. $2-3M of development later, I've made the mistakes so you don't have to."
