## GLOSSARY

| Acronym | Full Name | Definition |
|---------|-----------|------------|
| **RECA** | Razões Emocionais para Comprar Agora | Emotional reasons to buy now. The real decision driver — what the customer wants to *feel* (security, freedom, status, family wellbeing). |
| **RALOCA** | Razões Lógicas para Comprar Agora | Logical reasons to buy now. The rationalization the brain constructs *after* the emotional decision (features, specs, ROI). |
| **RADOVECA** | Razões Dominantes para Evitar a Compra | Dominant reasons to avoid the purchase. Objections and fears that block conversion. Must be anticipated and neutralized. |
| **DNB** | Dinheiro No Bolso | Real net profitability — the actual cash that sustains life, not gross revenue vanity metrics. |
| **IC** | Índice de Confiança ("Índice de Cagaço") | Confidence Index. The internal courage required to quote a high price. Fear of rejection is the single biggest block to high-ticket pricing. |

## THE BUYING BRAIN — FOUNDATION

> *"Nós não somos um ser racional. Nós somos um ser emocional que de vez em quando pensa."*
> — Everton Pieri

All purchasing decisions are driven by emotion first, logic second. The customer:
1. Decides emotionally (RECA)
2. Needs logical cover to feel comfortable with the decision (RALOCA)
3. Has fears and objections that can stop the purchase before it happens (RADOVECA)

The salesperson's job: activate RECA → provide RALOCA → neutralize RADOVECA.

---

## RECA — Razões Emocionais para Comprar Agora

### What It Is
RECA is the **real decision engine.** It lives at the Belief/Emotion level — universal human desires for security, peace, recognition, belonging, love, freedom, or family wellbeing.

> *"O cliente muitas vezes justifica a compra com lógica (RALOCA), mas a decisão é feita pelo RECA, que envolve desejos universais como segurança, paz interior, reconhecimento, ou o bem-estar da família."*

### The 4-Level Questioning Method

Descend through four levels to reach the emotional core:

#### Level 1 — Ambiente (Where?)
Map the situational context.
> *"Onde exatamente esse problema mais te atrapalha no dia a dia?"*

#### Level 2 — Comportamento (What?)
Focus on the specific symptom.
> *"O que exatamente está te travando ou te doendo hoje?"*

#### Level 3 — Capacidade (How?)
Explore the performance impact.
> *"Como esse problema está afetando seus resultados ou sua confiança?"*

#### Level 4 — Crença/Emoção (Why?) ← RECA lives here
> *"Por que é importante para você resolver isso agora?"*
> *"O que muda na sua vida se a gente conseguir esse resultado?"*

### The Disney Example
A client says they want to earn more money (surface). The RECA revealed at Level 4:
> *"Levar minha filha pra Disney."*

Everything in the presentation must anchor to that emotional destination — not the business metrics.

### Key Principle
> *"Vender bem é perguntar bem. Vender caro é perguntar ainda melhor — para extrair o RECA. Se você fica nas especificações técnicas, você alimenta o RALOCA e corre o risco de ser comparado por preço."*

## RALOCA — Razões Lógicas para Comprar Agora

### What It Is
RALOCA is **post-decision rationalization.** The brain needs a logical story to justify what it already decided emotionally.

RALOCA is what clients SAY they want. RECA is what they ACTUALLY want.

### RECA vs RALOCA Comparison

| Dimension | RALOCA | RECA |
|-----------|--------|------|
| What it is | Rational justification | Real emotional motor |
| Client says | "What's the price?", "What do I get?" | Rarely volunteers this spontaneously |
| Focus | On the product/service | On the client's life and identity |
| Function | Validates the decision | Motivates the decision |
| Question level | Environment, Behavior, Capability | Belief/Emotion (Why?) |

> **Warning:** Clients rarely deliver RECA immediately. They may say they want to lose weight (RALOCA surface) — the real RECA is *"feeling attractive again"* or *"playing with my kids without getting tired."*

### The Commodity Trap
Professionals who only address RALOCA (specs, features, deliverables) become commodities compared on price. The differentiation happens at the RECA level.

## RADOVECA — Razões Dominantes para Evitar a Compra

### What It Is
RADOVECA is the **objection cluster** — fears, doubts, and resistances that prevent conversion.

> *"Até 90% das objeções são falsas."*

The word "no" almost never means final rejection. It means:
- "I don't yet understand how this solves my specific problem"
- "I'm using money as a polite exit because I don't know how to say no"
- "I haven't yet seen enough evidence to trust this"

### The Money Objection — Brazil's Default
> *"Dinheiro não é o melhor motivo, mas é sempre a melhor desculpa."*

In Brazil, "não tenho dinheiro" is the culturally safe objection — most vendors don't know how to handle it.

**Proof it's usually false:** Most people have bought things without having the full money available (car, house, luxury item). The question is never "do they have money?" — it's "is this worth finding the money for?"

### 5-Part RADOVECA Neutralization System

#### 1. Reframe the "No"
The no is information, not rejection. It reveals what's missing: clarity, trust, or perceived value.
> *"O 'não' é quase sempre 'ainda não'."*

#### 2. Deploy Level 4 Questions
Run the RECA cascade again focused on the block:
> *"O que te impede de dar esse passo agora?"*
> *"Se o dinheiro não fosse questão, você avançaria?"*

#### 3. Calculate the Cost of Inaction
Make the pain of staying still more concrete than the fear of investing.

*The transcript reframe:*
> *"Você está faturando R$30k/mês há 3 anos querendo chegar em R$60k. Isso está te custando R$30k/mês. São R$1,08M nos últimos 3 anos. Quanto está custando não resolver isso? Não só financeiramente — emocionalmente, no casamento?"*

#### 4. Anticipate in the Presentation
Name the common objections BEFORE the client raises them.
> *"Nesse momento muita gente me pergunta sobre X — deixa eu já te responder isso antes."*

#### 5. Relentless Professional Follow-Up
If the client objected and didn't buy, maintain professional contact until they buy or explicitly opt out.

## THE 4-LEVEL INVESTIGATION FRAMEWORK

```
LEVEL 1 — AMBIENTE
"Onde esse problema aparece na sua vida/negócio?"
         ↓
LEVEL 2 — COMPORTAMENTO
"O que exatamente está acontecendo que está te travando?"
         ↓
LEVEL 3 — CAPACIDADE
"Como isso está afetando seus resultados?"
         ↓
LEVEL 4 — CRENÇA/EMOÇÃO ← RECA lives here
"Por que é importante pra você resolver isso AGORA?"
"O que muda na sua vida quando isso estiver resolvido?"
```

## THE 7-STEP CLEAR SALES MESSAGE

| Step | Element | What to do |
|------|---------|------------|
| 1 | **Desire** | Open with what the client wants. They must feel: "This person knows exactly what I want." |
| 2 | **Problem** | Identify the negative scenario they're living. Show you understand their pain better than they do. |
| 3 | **Guide** | Brief overcoming story (Empathy) + show your results (Authority). |
| 4 | **Plan** | Present a simple 3-4 step path. Use your method as the plan. |
| 5 | **Call to Action** | Firm, convicted invitation. Hesitation kills the sale. |
| 6 | **Success** | Life after the solution — speak to RECA, not RALOCA. |
| 7 | **Failure** | What they continue to lose by not deciding now. Cost of inaction. |

## TRANSFORMING TECHNICAL EXPERTISE INTO A METHOD

### The Core Error
> *"O erro do profissional técnico é vender o ingrediente (o botox, a aula, a planilha). O método vende a transformação."*

### Creating a Named Method
- ❌ Sell "Phenol Peel" (ugly process)
- ✅ Sell **"Time Machine"** (desirable rejuvenation outcome)

### Preço se Justifica com Percepção
> *"Preço não se justifica com entrega. O preço se justifica com percepção."*

Build the perception before revealing the price. A list of deliverables signals a generalist. Certainty of outcome signals a specialist.

## THE CONFIDENCE PILLAR

### Champion Salesperson's Triad

**1. Fome (Hunger)**
Visceral drive to win. Without it, no energy to seize opportunities.

**2. Técnica (Technique)**
RECA/RALOCA/RADOVECA + 4-level investigation + clear messaging.

**3. Gestão Emocional (Emotional Management)**
The capacity to absorb "no" after "no" without losing conviction. The IC — Índice de Confiança. Fear of quoting a high price is the single biggest block to high-ticket pricing.

> *"A maior barreira para cobrar caro é o medo interno do vendedor, não a resistência do cliente."*

## HIGH PRICE LOGIC

> *"É melhor ter 10 clientes pagando 10.000 do que 100 clientes pagando 1.000. O esforço de vendas é menor e o lucro é maior."*

> *"Quando você é muito mais caro, você coloca em cheque o mais barato — você gera dúvida sobre a concorrência."*

> *"Cliente novo não sabe o preço."* — Raise prices for new clients first. They have no reference point.
