## CORE THESIS: VENDER CARO É MAIS FÁCIL

**The single most important insight from Everton Pieri:**

> "Vender caro é mais fácil do que vender barato."

**Why this is true (from transcripts):**

1. **Price signals quality without any explanation.** When shown two items — one at R$500, one at R$5,000 — the brain automatically concludes the R$5,000 item is better, before any feature is described.

2. **Very good + very cheap = distrust.** "Muito bom e muito barato gera desconfiança. O cérebro odeia inconsistência." If something is great and cheap, the brain suspects a scam (same as when a husband suddenly brings coffee in bed — "what does he want?").

3. **Cheap clients are worse clients.** "Quanto mais barato teu cliente paga, pior ele é. Ele paga quase nada, ele reclama, ele quer tudo."

4. **The funnel math proves it:**
   - Charging R$3,000, 33% margin → R$1,000 profit per sale, 5 presentations per sale
   - Double the price to R$6,000, same delivery, ~R$3,000 profit
   - Even if you need 10 presentations at the higher price (2x worse), you still make more money with half the client volume

5. **"Cliente novo não sabe o preço."** New clients have no reference point. You define the anchor. Existing clients stay at old price — only new clients see the new price.

## DNB — DINHEIRO NO BOLSO

**Definition:** "Dinheiro no Bolso" is Everton's core business philosophy and the name of his flagship methodology. DNB = Real net profitability that actually sustains your life — not gross revenue, not vanity metrics.

**Core principle:** Strip away all self-deception about business success. The question is not "how much did I invoice?" but "how much is actually in my pocket at the end of the month?"

**DNB diagnostic questions:**
- What is your monthly net profit (not revenue)?
- What does your business cost to run?
- How many clients do you need at your current price to hit your DNB target?
- What happens if you halve the client count and double the price?

**Why DNB resonates:** Aesthetic professionals (Pieri's main audience) often invoice high but profit low — high costs, many clients, constant stress, zero margin. DNB reframes success around what actually lands.

## PRICE PSYCHOLOGY — THE BRAIN DECISION SYSTEM

### The Inconsistency Rule
The brain is built to detect inconsistency. When something is simultaneously "very good" AND "very cheap," the brain flags it as suspect.

**Practical implication:** Charging low prices when you deliver excellent results actively harms your sales, because you trigger the brain's fraud-detection mechanism.

### The R$500 vs R$5.000 Exercise
*Everton's live demonstration (used in presentations and podcasts):*

> "I have two things in my hands. One costs R$500, the other costs R$5,000. I'm going to give you one of them. Which do you choose?"

The audience always picks R$5,000 — without knowing any features, without any marketing, without any explanation.

**Why it works:** This exercise bypasses rational objections and reveals the emotional pricing truth: price IS the signal of quality before any other signal is processed.

### The Credibility Axis
- Below market price → "What's wrong with it?"
- Market price → "Okay, another option"
- Significantly above market → "Must be in a different league"

When you are the most expensive by a wide margin, you **shift the question from "why is this expensive?" to "why is everything else so cheap?"**

### The Specialist vs Generalist Distinction
*From the "R$150,000 vs R$20,000" story in the transcript:*

A competitor presented a R$20,000 proposal with an exhaustive list of deliverables. Everton presented R$150,000 with a focused, specialist approach.

The client said he expected R$300,000 (double Everton's price). Everton's response:
> "Para custar só o dobro, eu teria que ser só duas vezes melhor. Eu teria que piorar muito e não consigo."

**Rule:** A generalist justifies price through volume of deliverables. A specialist justifies price through certainty of outcome.

## OBJECTION FRAMEWORK

### The 90% Rule
> "Até 90% das objeções são falsas."

Most objections are not the real reason someone isn't buying. The brain knows the vendor expects to hear "no money" and uses it because it's the easiest exit.

### The Money Objection Reframe
When a prospect says "I don't have the money":
1. **Acknowledge** — Don't argue
2. **Calculate the cost of inaction** — "If you've been at R$30k/month for 3 years wanting R$60k, how much has NOT solving this cost you? 3 years × R$30k opportunity gap = R$1M lost."
3. **Reframe** — "Dinheiro não é o melhor motivo, mas é sempre a melhor desculpa."
4. **Show the ROI** — A mentoring that doubles revenue pays for itself in the first month

### The "Cliente Novo Não Sabe o Preço" Tactic
When raising prices:
- Don't change existing clients — they stay at old rates
- New clients only see new rates
- New clients have no reference point for what you "used to charge"
- Raise prices immediately for all incoming prospects

## THE MENTOR VALUE STACK

*From the R$140,000 mentoring discussion (BNze7NdVoU8 transcript):*

1. **Tempo (Time)** — Compress years of trial and error into months

## BUSINESS MINDSET PRINCIPLES

### O Negócio É Reflexo do Dono
> "O negócio é reflexo do dono. Se o CPF não tá bem, o CNPJ não vai estar bem."

The owner's internal state — beliefs, emotional patterns, self-worth — directly determines the business's ceiling. Business problems are usually personal problems in disguise.

### Pessoas Curadas Curam Gerações
> "Pessoas curadas curam gerações."

Transformation is generational. When you fix the person, they fix their business, their family, their environment. This is the deeper mission beyond business consulting.

### Crença vs Comportamento
> "O que é mais fácil mudar: uma crença ou um comportamento?"

Everton's provocative insight: the market says beliefs are hard to change. But in reality, **behavior changes first** — beliefs follow. You don't need to "fix your money beliefs" before raising prices. Raise prices. The belief updates after the behavior.

**Application:** Stop waiting for internal permission to charge more. Start charging more. The evidence from new clients will shift the belief.

### O Estresse Não É O Trabalho, É A Recompensa
> "Não é ganhar pouco que estressa. É você trabalhar muito e não ter nenhuma recompensa disso."

Stress doesn't come from hard work. It comes from hard work without commensurate reward. High-ticket eliminates this pattern by ensuring your effort is properly compensated.

## CRITICAL WARNINGS

1. **Justify price with deliverables:** Listing many deliverables signals generalist, not specialist. The R$20k competitor lost the deal by itemizing more. Less = more confidence.

2. **Moving existing clients to new price:** Don't. Raise price for new clients only. "Cliente novo não sabe o preço."

3. **Waiting for permission to charge more:** Behavior precedes belief. Raise price now; the internal permission will follow.

4. **Confusing invoice with profit:** DNB is net, not gross. A R$100k/month clinic with R$90k costs is a R$10k business in DNB terms.

5. **Ignoring the brain's inconsistency detector:** Cheap + good = distrust. This is not an opinion — it's how buying decisions work neurologically.
