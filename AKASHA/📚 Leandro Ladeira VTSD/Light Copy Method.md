## CORE DEFINITION

**Light Copy** is a persuasion approach that is light, authentic, fun, and genuinely conversational — but converts consistently. It rejects heavy, exaggerated, pressure-driven copy in favor of honest premises, relatable stories, and natural language that feels like a conversation between people who trust each other.

**Key contrast:**
| Heavy Copy | Light Copy |
|-----------|-----------|
| Screams at the reader | Talks with the reader |
| Exaggerated promises | Honest premises |
| High pressure, urgency tricks | Natural curiosity and connection |
| Corporate/formal tone | Casual, human, direct |
| Announces | Converses |

## FUNDAMENTAL CONCEPTS

### THE THREE PILLARS OF LIGHT COPY

**Pillar 1 — Copy Must Sound Like a Conversation**
- Read your copy aloud. If you wouldn't say it to a person's face, rewrite it
- No formal language, no corporate jargon, no "Dear reader"
- Short sentences. Natural rhythm. Contractions. Questions

**Pillar 2 — Never Underestimate Your Audience**
- The modern reader is saturated with marketing
- Treating them as intelligent builds trust faster than any tactic
- When you respect their intelligence, they lower their guard

**Pillar 3 — The Conversation Must Be Light**
- Lightness = effortless to read, no cognitive load
- One idea per paragraph maximum
- White space is persuasion tool — use it

### MARKETING DE PREMISSAS vs. MARKETING DE PROMESSAS
**Definition:** The most important distinction in modern copywriting.

| Marketing de Promessas | Marketing de Premissas |
|----------------------|----------------------|
| "Lose 10kg in 30 days!" | "Here's why most diets fail on day 3" |
| Makes bold claims | States undeniable truths |
| Creates skepticism | Creates agreement |
| Saturated market | Stands out immediately |
| Sells before trust is earned | Builds trust that enables selling |

**Core principle:** Start with something your reader already believes is true. Build from agreement. The sale happens naturally from accumulated agreement.

**Application for Runa Systems:** Instead of "AI will transform your business" → "You already know that manual tasks are eating hours you could spend on what actually grows your business."

---

### SENTIMENTO (FEAR / GREED)
**Definition:** All purchasing decisions are driven by two emotional engines.

| Engine | What triggers it | Copy approach |
|--------|-----------------|---------------|
| **Fear (Medo)** | Fear of loss, fear of being left behind, fear of making mistakes | "What happens if you don't..." |
| **Greed (Ganância)** | Desire for gain, improvement, status | "Imagine what's possible when..." |

**Rule:** Never use both at the same time — pick the dominant emotion for your audience and go deep on it.

---

### GATILHOS MENTAIS (MENTAL TRIGGERS)
**Definition:** Psychological mechanisms that reduce cognitive friction and accelerate decisions.

**Core triggers (Light Copy application):**

| Trigger | Description | Light application |
|---------|-------------|------------------|
| **Inimigo em comum** | Shared enemy creates instant tribe | "We both hate when [common frustration]..." |
| **Autoridade** | Demonstrated expertise | Show results and process, not just credentials |
| **Prova social** | Others doing it removes doubt | Specific, named examples — not generic testimonials |
| **Reciprocidade** | Give first, genuinely | Deliver real value before the ask |
| **Escassez real** | Genuine limits create urgency | Only use when real — never manufactured |
| **Novidade** | New mechanism creates curiosity | "The thing nobody is talking about yet..." |
| **Pertencimento** | Belonging to a group | Community language, insider references |

### ESTUDO DO AVATAR
**Definition:** Deep research into the exact person you're writing for. Not demographics — psychographics.

**Avatar research questions:**
- What do they secretly fear about their current situation?
- What would their ideal Tuesday look like?
- What have they already tried that didn't work?
- What do they tell themselves is the reason they're stuck?
- What would they be embarrassed to admit in public?

**Rule:** The more specific your avatar, the more universal your copy. Paradox of specificity.

### ARQUÉTIPOS (ARCHETYPES)
**Definition:** Universal character patterns that trigger instinctive recognition and connection.

**Key archetypes for Brazilian digital market:**

| Archetype | Characteristics | Best used for |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| **O Herói** | Overcomes adversity, earns the result | Transformation stories |
| **O Sábio** | Knowledge, teaches the way | Education/authority content |

| **O Cuidador** | Protects, guides, nurtures | Services, communities |
| **O Explorador** | Discovers, pioneers | New method/approach content |

### ADVANCED COPY TECHNIQUES

#### Sentir na Pele (Feel It on Your Skin)
Make the reader *feel* the situation through sensory language. Not "you'll have more time" but "imagine it's 3pm on a Tuesday and you have nowhere to be."

#### Sentir no Corpo (Feel It in the Body)
Physical sensations trigger emotional memory. "That tightness in your chest when you check the bank balance." Reader's body responds before their mind does.

#### Criação de Personagem (Character Creation)
Every effective piece of copy has a character the reader follows. The character doesn't need to be you — can be a client, a composite, a type.

#### Metáfora (Metaphor)
The fastest way to explain a complex concept. One good metaphor replaces three paragraphs of explanation.

#### Setup + Punch
Comedy structure applied to copy. Build expectation → subvert it.
- Setup: establishes a pattern
- Punch: breaks it in a surprising but logical way
Result: attention, memorability, shareability

#### Antítese
Contrasting two ideas in the same sentence to create tension and clarity.
- "Most people work hard to have less. We're building a system to have more by working less."

#### Linguagem Fantasiosa (Imaginative Language)
Invites the reader into a mental simulation of the desired outcome.
"Close your eyes. You wake up, check your phone — 3 new client messages overnight. You didn't send a single DM."

#### Superstição (Superstition)
Leverages existing beliefs or cultural narratives the reader already holds.

#### Sempre Que (Whenever)
Pattern-interrupt trigger: "Whenever you feel [X], it means [unexpected reframe]."

#### Afeição Médio (Medium Affection)
Not too formal, not too intimate. The tone of a smart, trusted friend — not a best friend, not a stranger.

### IMPACTO VISUAL
**Definition:** The visual appearance of copy affects how it's processed before a word is read.

**Rules:**
- Short paragraphs (2-3 lines max for social/email)
- Strategic white space
- Bold only what truly matters — if everything is bold, nothing is
- Line breaks at natural speech pauses
- Never justify text alignment — left align reads faster

---

### QUAL É O PIOR MOMENTO PARA ESCREVER COPY?
**Answer:** When you need to sell. Write copy when you have nothing at stake — then use it when you need it.

**Principle:** The desperation to sell leaks through the words. Build a copy bank. Write when in a good mental state; deploy strategically.

## COPY FRAMEWORK: THE LIGHT STRUCTURE

```
HOOK     — Stop the scroll (pattern interrupt or bold premise)
     ↓
PREMISE  — State something undeniably true they already believe
     ↓
TENSION  — Create the gap between where they are and where they want to be
     ↓
BRIDGE   — You/your method as the logical path between gap and desire
     ↓
EVIDENCE — Specific proof (not generic claims)
     ↓
CTA      — Light ask, natural next step
```

## CRITICAL WARNINGS
1. **Promising without premise:** Starting with "Imagine making R$50k/month" immediately triggers skepticism. Start with what they already believe.
2. **Over-formatting:** Excessive bold, caps, emojis — signals insecurity about the content itself
3. **Copying tone without understanding avatar:** Light Copy sounds different for each specific avatar. Study before writing.
4. **Ignoring the visual layer:** Great copy in a wall of text won't convert. Layout is part of the message.
